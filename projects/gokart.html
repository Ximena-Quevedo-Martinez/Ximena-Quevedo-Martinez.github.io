Electric Go-Kart

A personal hands-on bachelor project: an electric go-kart driven by a 24 V DC motor and controlled by a PIC18F4550 microcontroller.

  • Role Personal project
  • Domain Embedded · Power electronics
  • Platform PIC18F4550
  • Stack Embedded C

The challenge

Turn classroom theory into a vehicle that actually drives, built and wired by hand during my bachelor's.

The approach

A 24 V DC-motor go-kart controlled by a PIC18F4550, with a graphic LCD for vehicle status and an ultrasonic sensor for obstacle detection.

1

Power

24 V DC motor and its power electronics

2

Control

PIC18F4550 microcontroller in embedded C

3

Status

Graphic LCD showing the vehicle state

4

Sense

Ultrasonic obstacle detection

Why it mattered

The first time I turned theory into a working vehicle, end to end.

Hands-on scope

Mechanics, power electronics, firmware and the HMI, all in one build.

Result

A driveable electric go-kart I built end to end, from power electronics and microcontroller firmware to the LCD interface and obstacle sensing, and the project where I first became an engineer who builds working things.
